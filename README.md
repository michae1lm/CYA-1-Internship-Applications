## 6. Reflection

For this activity, the expectation was to complete a career-aligned experience and reflect on the process. I chose to apply to cybersecurity internships because it directly supports my goal of entering the cybersecurity field.

One key decision I made was to apply to a variety of roles, including penetration testing, data security, and government positions. I did this to explore different paths within cybersecurity and understand which areas I am most interested in.

I worked independently on this activity. My contributions included researching positions, preparing application materials, submitting applications, and reviewing interview preparation strategies.

I think I performed well in terms of effort and consistency, especially by applying to multiple positions and spending time preparing. However, if I were to improve, I would spend more time tailoring my resume and gaining more hands-on technical experience to strengthen my applications.

This experience showed me how competitive cybersecurity internships are and what skills employers expect, such as networking knowledge, security tools, and practical experience. It also reinforced the importance of continuing to build skills and work toward certifications like Security+.

Overall, this activity moved me closer to my career goals by giving me real exposure to the application process and helping me understand what I need to improve going forward.
